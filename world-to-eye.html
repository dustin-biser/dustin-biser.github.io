World Space to Eye Space Transformation

Here we explain and derive the transformations responsible for transforming vectors and points from our World Coordinate System $WCS$ to our Eye Coordinate System $ECS$ (also know as the Camera Coordinate System). The $ECS$-to-$WCS$ transformation is a rather integral part of the full graphics transformation process, which in total is responsible for mapping vectors and points from object space all the way to screen space.

The conversion from $WCS$ to $ECS$ can be accomplished by applying two transformations in the following order:

  1. Translating by the distance between the two spaces so their origins coincide.
  2. Applying a Change of Basis from one coordinate system to the other.

Note that steps 1 and 2 are needed for transforming points from $WCS$ to $ECS$, but to transform vectors, all that is needed is the Change of Basis step. This comes form the fact that vectors have direction and magnitude, but no position.

The translation step will involve moving all points given with respect to the $WCS$ a distance and direction equal to moving the $ECS$ origin so it coincides with the $WCS$ origin. The location of the $ECS$ origin can be described in $WCS$ components to give $[\vec{E}]_{WCS} = [E_x,E_y,E_z]^T$. Using the components of this vector, we can construct a translation matrix that will move our points to the desired location. \[ \left[ \begin{array}{ccc} 1 & 0 & 0 & -E_x \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & -E_y \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & -E_z \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \end{array} \right]\] This takes care of the translation part, now we need to deal with Change of Basis step. Assume that $ECS$ is composed of the basis vectors $\{\hat{x_e}, \hat{y_e}, \hat{z_e}\}$, which make up its unit axes.

Each basis vector of $ECS$ can be decomposed into its own combination of $WCS$ components given as follows: \begin{eqnarray*} [\hat{x_e}]_{WCS} & = [a_x, a_y, a_z]^T \\ [\hat{y_e}]_{WCS} & = [b_x, b_y, b_z]^T \\ [\hat{z_e}]_{WCS} & = [c_x, c_y, c_z]^T \end{eqnarray*} Since the unit vectors of $WCS$ and $ECS$ separately form a basis of $\mathbb{R}^3$, there exists a unique matrix $\underset{WCS \leftarrow ECS}{P}$ such that given any vector $\vec{u} \in \mathbb{R}^3$ \begin{equation*} [\vec{u}]_{WCS} = \underset{WCS \leftarrow ECS}{P} [\vec{u}]_{ECS} \end{equation*} The matrix $\underset{WCS \leftarrow ECS}{P}$ is called the change of basis matrix from $WCS$ to $ECS$.
